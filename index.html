About This Workshop

This workshop provides participants with practical tools and checklists for conducting rigorous, transparent peer review of observational and interventional research in obstetric anaesthesia and perinatology. The resources below align with current reporting standards (CONSORT, STROBE) and SOAP editorial expectations.

Peer Review Quick Tips

  1. 01
    Evaluate the study design first. Confirm the design matches the research question before assessing the statistics.
  2. 02
    Use the appropriate reporting checklist. CONSORT for RCTs, STROBE for observational studies, PRISMA for systematic reviews.
  3. 03
    Assess clinical significance, not just statistical significance. A p-value alone does not determine importance; look at effect sizes and confidence intervals.
  4. 04
    Be constructive and specific. Vague comments delay revision. Point to line numbers or tables and suggest improvements.
  5. 05
    Declare conflicts of interest. If you recognise the authors or have competing interests, notify the editor promptly.
